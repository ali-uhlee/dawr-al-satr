Overview

Dawr al-Satr means "Period of Concealment" when the Imam was hidden due to political danger. The Imamate continued through representatives instead of public leadership.

Timeline

765 CE – Death of Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq
Late 700s – Concealment begins
800s – Expansion of da'wa network
909 CE – Fatimid Caliphate established

Key Figures

Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq
His death led to division and secrecy.
Muhammad ibn Ismail
Associated with beginning of concealment.
Ubayd Allah al-Mahdi
Founded the Fatimid Caliphate.

Importance

Preserved the Imamate during danger
Built strong global networks
Allowed future re-emergence
Showed adaptability of leadership
