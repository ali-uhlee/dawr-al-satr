Overview

Dawr al-Satr refers to a period when the Imam remained hidden due to political threats. Leadership continued through trusted representatives, maintaining continuity despite danger.

Timeline

765 CE – Death of Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq
Late 700s – Concealment begins
800s – Da'wa network expands
909 CE – Fatimid Caliphate established

Key Figures

Ja'far al-Sadiq – Foundation of knowledge
Muhammad ibn Ismail – Beginning of concealment
Ubayd Allah al-Mahdi – Re-emergence and state formation

Importance

Preserved leadership during danger
Built global networks
Allowed re-emergence
Demonstrated adaptability
